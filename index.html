Researcher with a strong background in physics, mathematics, and numerical modelling, currently expanding into machine learning, and exploring physics-informed neural networks. Seeking research opportunities outside academia to address real-world challenges.

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the University of Nottingham and a member of the Particle Cosmology group and the Nottingham Center of Gravity. My main research is numerical simulations of gravitational systems. It combines theoretical knowledge of gravitation and partial differential equations, with numerical analysis, software development, and data analysis. One of my recent research projects includes SpheriCo.jl, a code for gravitational collapse in semiclassical gravity. In the past, my work was focused on the characteristic problem of general relativity, specifically the mathematical properties of hyperbolicity and well-posedness, the numerical convergence, as well as some of its applications. You can find more about my work on: InspireHEP, ORCID, google scholar, github, LinkedIn.
